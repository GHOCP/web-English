Apology

➀ Say sorry to someone you have hurt

❇ ❇ ❇ 
I beg your pardon. Used in polite speech to apologize when one has done something impolite or has made a mistake. I apologize. This is the simplest way to apologize for something you did wrong. My apologies. Same as above.
My bad. This one's slang and should only be used with friends or people your age. It's very casual and generally used to apologize for a minor mistake. I'm very/truly/terribly/deeply sorry. Use when you're aware that you've hurt someone and want to genuinely apologize. I'm so sorry about that. Same as above.
I'm so sorry about that. Same as above. I'm so sorry, that was thoughtless of me. Use it if you've hurt someone by doing something thoughtlessly. I'm sorry, that was wrong of me. Use it when you know did something wrong.
I was wrong about that. You can use this expression to admit you've made a mistake. I'm sorry, that was my fault. Use it to admit that you've made a mistake that hurt someone. I'm very sorry for the way I behaved. Use this if you behaved rudely and want to apologize.
I apologize. I was out of line. Same as above. You can blame me for that. With this expression, you admit it was your fault and take the blame. How should I apologize to you? With this expression, you're asking someone to indicate how to best apologize to them.
I want to say I'm sorry / I want to tell you I'm sorry. A polite way to say you're sorry. My humble apologies. 对不起
