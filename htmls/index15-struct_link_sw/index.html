ADV(-ly)

WORDSMEANINGSENTENCE
Supposedly /səˈpoʊzɪdli/ adv. according to what is generally thought or believed but not known for certain 据信；据传；据说 The novel is supposedly based on a true story. 据说这部小说是以一个真实的故事为依据的。
Allegedly /əˈledʒɪdli/ adv. 据说，据宣称 His van allegedly struck the two as they were crossing a street. 据称，他的货车在这两个人过马路时撞上了他们。
Justifiably /ˌdʒʌstɪˈfaɪəbli/ adv. 言之有理地， 无可非议地 He was justifiably proud of his achievements. 他无可非议地为自己的成就感到骄傲。
Invariably /ɪnˈveriəbli/ adv. = without fail: always This acute infection of the brain is almost invariably fatal. 这种急性大脑传染病几乎总是导致死亡。
Admittedly used, especially at the beginning of a sentence, when you are accepting that sth is true （尤用于句首）诚然，无可否认 Admittedly, it is rather expensive but you don't need to use much. 它的确很贵，但不需要用得很多。
Undeservedly ˌʌndɪˈzɜːrvɪdli/ adv. 不应得地，不当地 -- "I'm a lucky man, undeservedly lucky," he said humbly.
-- Many subsidies undeservedly survived the cull. 许多补贴不当地躲过剔除。
Unwittingly /ʌnˈwɪtɪŋli/ adv. without being aware of what you are doing or the situation that you are involved in 糊里糊涂地；茫然；无意地 She had broken the law unwittingly, but still she had broken it. 她并非故意犯法，但毕竟是犯了法。
Solely /ˈsoʊlli/ adv. only; not involving sb/sth else 仅；只；唯；单独地 She was motivated solely by self-interest. 她完全是出于私利。
Arguably adv. used, often before a comparative or superlative adjective, when you are stating an opinion which you believe you could give reasons to support （常用于形容词比较级或最高级前）可论证地，按理 He is arguably the best actor of his generation. 按理他是他那一代中最优秀的演员。
Comparably ˈkɑmpərəbli/ adv. in a similar way or to a similar degree a comparably priced CD player 同等价格的激光唱机
Ostensibly /ɑːˈstensəbli/ adv. 表面上；外表 Troops were sent in, ostensibly to protect the civilian population. 谎称为保护平民而派驻了军队。
Legitimately /lɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli/ adv. 合法地；合理地；正当地，合乎逻辑地 -- She can now legitimately claim to be the best in the world. 现在她可以理所当然地声称自己是世界上最优秀的。
-- A question we can legitimately raise. 一个可以合理提出的问题。
Collectively adv. 集体地，共同地 -- They collectively decided to recognize the changed situation.
-- Painting sculpture architecture etc. are collectively called the "fine arts" or "plastic arts".
Implicitly 1. 暗中地，含蓄地；
2. 绝对地

Others

STRUCTUREMEANINGSENTENCE
Whereby /weəˈbaɪ/ adv. by which; because of which 凭此；借以；由于 They have introduced a new system whereby all employees must undergo regular training.
Whatsoever adv. /ˌwʌtsoʊˈevər/ used to emphasize a negative statement 丝毫〔用于强调否定句〕
conj.（古 whatever）		 He's had no luck whatsoever. 他一点也不走运。
Scarce /skeəs/ adv. (literary) only just; almost not 勉强；刚；几乎不；简直不 I can scarce remember him. 我几乎想不起他了。
Thereof /ˌðeərˈɒv/ adv. (formal) (law 律) of the thing mentioned 在其中；由此 Is the property or any part thereof used for commercial activity? 这一房产或其中任何部分有用于商业活动吗？
Granted adv. used to show that you accept that sth is true, often before you make another statement about it（表示肯定属实，然后再作另一番表述）不错，的确 Granted, it's not the most pleasant of jobs but it has to be done. 的确，这不是最令人愉快的工作，但也得做呀。
Seemingly(1) adv. in a way that appears to be true but may in fact not be -- a seemingly stupid question 看似愚蠢的问题
-- a seemingly endless journey 似乎永远走不完的路
Seemingly(2) adv. according to what you have read or heard 据说；听说；看来 Seemingly, he borrowed the money from the bank. 据说，他从银行贷出了那笔钱。
Henceforth adv. = henceforward 此后；从此以后 Friday 31 July 1925 henceforth became known as ‘Red Friday’. 1925年7月31日这个星期五从此以后就称为“红色星期五”。
